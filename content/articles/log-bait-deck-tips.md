## 10 Log Bait Tips for Maximum Baited Value

![Log Bait deck](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse2.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.2qE66Y9IB729GlXZM0uLewHaEK%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=5fda7ae31982fc6bc241f851f8233a45e431e6d263514bca2dd010b2483fb5b0&ipo=images)

Log Bait is the deck where you win not by overpowering your opponent — but by making them choose wrong. Every card in your deck demands their Log. The skill is sequencing so they never have it when you need it gone.

---

### 1. The Three Barrel Placements

Goblin Barrel (3 elixir) has three placements and each counters a different response. **Center placement** (on top of tower): Goblins surround the tower, 2 survive Zap, all 3 survive Log if timed late. **Front placement** (in front of tower): Goblins land closer to the bridge, harder to spell, ideal when they pre-Log. **Deep placement** (behind tower): Goblins land deep, bypassing pre-placed troops at the bridge. Rotate all three. If you use the same placement twice, good players pre-Log and kill all three Goblins for +1 trade.

### 2. Princess at the Bridge: When and Why

Princess (3 elixir) at the bridge applies instant pressure: her first shot hits the tower for 140 damage before they can react. BUT: if they have Log in cycle, you just fed them +1 elixir and your Princess is dead. The rule: only bridge-Princess when their Log is OUT of cycle (they just used it). Track their Log. Bridge Princess = guaranteed 140-280 chip damage. Back Princess = safe, slow value. The difference between the two is knowing their cycle.

### 3. The Triple-Spell Bait Sequence

Classic Log Bait runs Goblin Barrel (Log target), Princess (Log target), and Goblin Gang (Log/Zap target). The sequence: play Princess at the back. They Log her. Now play Goblin Gang at the bridge. They Zap (or Snowball). Now play Goblin Barrel. They have neither Log nor Zap. Goblins connect for ~700 damage. The sequence works because you baited two spells before committing your win condition. Reverse the order and Barrel dies to Log.

### 4. Inferno Tower Placement: The Anti-Spell Position

Standard Inferno Tower placement (4 tiles from river, centered) is vulnerable to Earthquake, Lightning, and Rocket. The anti-spell position: 3 tiles from the river, 2 tiles off-center. This position still pulls both-lane tanks but is outside the overlapping spell zones where Lightning + Zap kill it. The cost: slightly less pull range. The benefit: your Inferno Tower survives spells that would one-shot it in the standard position.

### 5. Knight Is Your Win Condition in Disguise

In Log Bait, Knight (3 elixir) is not just a mini-tank — he is often the card that wins the game. A Knight at full HP walking toward their tower after killing a Mega Minion demands a response. They drop Goblins — dead to Princess. They drop Skeleton Army — dead. They drop Musketeer — Knight tanks, Goblins chip. The counter-push Knight with 1,200+ HP is a 3-elixir win condition. Treat him like one.

### 6. Rocket Cycle: The Clock Is Ticking

Rocket (6 elixir) deals 592 tower damage at tournament standard. When their tower is below 1,200 HP, you can Rocket it twice and win without ever crossing the bridge. In double elixir, cycle Rocket with cheap cards: Ice Spirit (1) + Skeletons (1) + Log (2) + Rocket (6) = 10 elixir = one cycle. At double speed, that is every ~14 seconds. Two rockets in 28 seconds = 1,184 damage. Unstoppable. The Rocket cycle is Log Bait's plan B — and it is unbeatable when the clock is under 60 seconds.

### 7. Ice Spirit + Goblin Barrel: The 4-Elixir Chip

Ice Spirit (1) at the bridge + Goblin Barrel (3) immediately behind. The Ice Spirit reaches the tower first. If they Log, the Ice Spirit tanks the Log and freezes the tower, Goblins connect. If they Skeleton Army, Ice Spirit freezes the Skeletons, Barrel lands, Goblins kill everything. Total cost: 4 elixir. Average damage: 400-700. This is the most efficient offensive play in Log Bait and the one that wins ladder matches below 6,000.

### 8. Defensive Goblin Barrel Is Real

Goblin Barrel is not just offense. Dropping Barrel on a Musketeer at the bridge kills her in 2.3 seconds (3 Goblins × 109 DPS = 327 DPS). Dropping Barrel on a lone Wizard kills him. Dropping Barrel on a distracted Mega Knight with tower support deals serious damage. The 3-elixir defensive Barrel is a surprise play that players at 5,000-6,000 trophies never expect. Use it once per match. It will win you games.

### 9. The Goblin Gang Surround

Goblin Gang (3 elixir) spawns 3 Goblins + 2 Spear Goblins. The surround placement on defense — tapping 1 tile BEHIND their tank — splits the Gang so splash troops (Wizard, Baby Dragon) only hit 2-3 of them. The Spear Goblins on the far side survive and continue dealing 112 DPS. A 3-elixir Gang that survives a Wizard shot with 3 surviving units is a +5 value defensive play. The surround is the difference between Gang dying instantly and Gang becoming a counter-push.

### 10. Know When to Abandon the Bait

If your opponent runs triple spell (Log + Arrows + Zap), the bait game is dead. They can answer every bait card. In this matchup, do not play Princess at the bridge. Do not Barrel aggressively. Switch to Rocket cycle. Defend. Rocket their tower on cooldown. The triple-spell deck has less offensive pressure (they invested 3 card slots in spells). Your defense holds. Your Rocket wins. Recognizing the unwinnable bait game and pivoting to Rocket is the skill that takes Log Bait from 6,000 to 7,000+.

---

## Key Takeaway

Log Bait is poker, not chess. You are not outplaying their cards — you are outplaying their decisions. Make them use their spells on the wrong targets, then punish with the right one. Every match is a mind game. Win it.
