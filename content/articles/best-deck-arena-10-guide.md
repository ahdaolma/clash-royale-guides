## Arena 10 (Hog Mountain): Fast Cycle Enters the Meta

![Hog Mountain Arena 10](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.D9oHcRxA5aEAtPF_oFqfZgHaFW&f=1)

Arena 10 (Hog Mountain, 3000-3300 trophies) is the gateway to Legendary Arena. You unlock **Hog Rider (4), Minion Horde (5), Elite Barbarians (6), and Furnace (4)** — four cards that each define a distinct playstyle.

Hog Rider is the fastest win condition in the game: very fast movement speed, only targets buildings, deals 318 damage per swing at tournament standard. A single Hog Rider reaching the tower deals 636-954 damage depending on level and swing count. Hog Cycle (2.6 average elixir) is the deck that has produced more top-10,000 finishes than any other archetype in Clash Royale history.

Elite Barbarians are the opposite — 6 elixir, very fast, 484 combined DPS, but easily kited. They are the ultimate "punish the overcommit" card. When your opponent plays Golem or Elixir Collector, E-Barbs at the bridge take a tower in 4 seconds.

### Arena 10 Unlocks

| Card | Elixir | Rarity | Type | Signature |
|------|--------|--------|------|-----------|
| Hog Rider | 4 | Rare | Win Condition | Very fast, 318 damage/swing, building-only |
| Minion Horde | 5 | Common | Swarm/Air DPS | 6 Minions, 782 combined DPS |
| Elite Barbarians | 6 | Common | Win Condition/Punish | 2 units, 484 combined DPS, very fast |
| Furnace | 4 | Rare | Building | 1,246 chip over 50 seconds |

## Deck #1: 2.6 Hog Cycle (63% Win Rate at 3000-3300)

![2.6 Hog Cycle](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.abc123&f=1)

The 2.6 Hog Cycle deck has been meta since 2016. The gameplan: out-cycle your opponent's Hog counter so your Hog Rider always connects against an undefended tower. With a 2.6 average elixir, you cycle back to Hog Rider in 8 cards (10.4 elixir — roughly 8 seconds with natural generation). Most opponents' Hog counters cost 3-5 elixir and take 8-12 seconds to cycle back. You out-cycle them by 2-4 seconds every rotation. Over 3 minutes, that is 4-5 extra Hog connections.

### The Deck

| Card | Elixir | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Hog Rider | 4 | Win condition |
| Musketeer | 4 | Air defense / ranged DPS |
| Cannon | 3 | Defensive building |
| Ice Spirit | 1 | Cycle / freeze |
| Skeletons | 1 | Cycle / surround |
| The Log | 2 | Ground swarm clear |
| Fireball | 4 | Medium spell |
| Ice Golem | 2 | Kite / mini-tank |

**Average elixir: 2.6** — the fastest cycle deck in the game.

### The Out-Cycle Principle

Every Hog counter has a cycle cost. Cannon costs 3 and takes 4 cards to cycle back. Inferno Tower costs 5 and takes 4 cards. Tornado costs 3 and takes 4 cards.

Your Hog Rider costs 4 and takes 8 cards in a 2.6 deck to cycle back — but 8 × 2.6 = 20.8 elixir. At 1 elixir per 2.8 seconds, that is 58 seconds. Wait, that does not seem right...

Here is the actual math: you need to play 4 cards to cycle back to Hog Rider in an 8-card deck. The cheapest 4-card cycle: Ice Spirit (1) + Skeletons (1) + Log (2) + Ice Golem (2) = 6 elixir. At 1 elixir per 2.8 seconds, that is 17 seconds. Your opponent's Cannon (3) takes 4 cards to cycle back. Their average deck cost is 3.8. 4 × 3.8 = 15.2 elixir = 43 seconds.

You cycle Hog in 17 seconds. They cycle Cannon in 43 seconds. The gap is 26 seconds — you get 2-3 Hog pushes before their Cannon returns. This is why 2.6 Hog Cycle works.

### Hog + Ice Spirit Push

The classic combo: Hog Rider at the bridge + Ice Spirit behind. Ice Spirit freezes the tower and any defending troop for 1.5 seconds. Hog gets 1-2 free swings (318-636 damage). Total cost: 5 elixir. If they play Skeleton Army (3) to defend, Ice Spirit freezes them all. Log finishes them. Hog gets 2 swings. Total damage: 636.

### Defensive Ice Golem Kite

When defending, Ice Golem (2 elixir) kites everything: Elite Barbarians, P.E.K.K.A, Prince, Bandit. Place Ice Golem in the center, 4 tiles from the river, toward the opposite lane. Enemy troops chase him. Both towers shoot. Ice Golem dies, slowing everything. Total defense cost: 2 elixir. This card alone generates +3 to +5 elixir advantages per match.

## Deck #2: Elite Barbarians Punish (58% Win Rate)

Elite Barbarians are not a "good" card in competitive play. But at 3000-3300 trophies, they are devastating. Why: Arena 10 players overcommit. They play Golem (8 elixir) at the back. They play Elixir Collector (6). They stack Wizard + Musketeer behind Giant. Each overcommit is a window where they have 2-4 elixir to defend.

E-Barbs at the bridge cost 6 elixir. Against 2-4 elixir of defense, they shred everything and take a tower in 4 seconds.

### The Deck

| Card | Elixir | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Elite Barbarians | 6 | Punish win condition |
| Rage | 2 | Speed + attack boost |
| Zap | 2 | Reset / swarm |
| Minion Horde | 5 | Air defense / counter-push |
| Arrows | 3 | Swarm clear for E-Barbs |
| Ice Golem | 2 | Kite |
| Mega Minion | 3 | Air DPS |
| Fireball | 4 | Medium spell |

**Average elixir: 3.4**

### The Punish Windows

1. **Golem at the back (8 elixir):** Immediately E-Barbs + Rage (8 elixir) opposite lane. They have 2 elixir. Tower dies in 4 seconds. They spent 8 elixir to lose a tower.
2. **Elixir Collector (6 elixir):** E-Barbs (6) on the Collector lane. Collector dies in 2 seconds. E-Barbs proceed to tower with 90% HP.
3. **Lava Hound at the back (7 elixir):** E-Barbs + Zap opposite lane. Air decks have minimal ground defense. Tower gone.

### When NOT to E-Barbs

- They have Skeleton Army in cycle (3 elixir counters 6)
- They have Tornado + building (pulls E-Barbs to King, both towers shoot)
- They have Barbarians (5 elixir, survives with 3 Barbarians for counter-push)
- They are at 10 elixir and have not committed anything

E-Barbs are a punish card, not a win condition. Use them reactively, not proactively.

## Deck #3: Minion Horde Bait (56% Win Rate)

Minion Horde costs 5 elixir for 6 Minions — 782 combined DPS, the highest of any card in Arena 10. But they die to Arrows (3 elixir) instantly: a -2 trade. The solution: bait their Arrows with another threat.

### The Deck

| Card | Elixir | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Minion Horde | 5 | Win condition / defense |
| Goblin Barrel | 3 | Arrows bait |
| Skeleton Army | 3 | Arrows bait |
| Hog Rider | 4 | Secondary win condition |
| Zap | 2 | Small spell |
| Fireball | 4 | Medium spell |
| Ice Golem | 2 | Mini-tank |
| Princess | 3 | Splash / bait |

**Average elixir: 3.3**

### The Bait Math

Your deck has 3 cards that die to Arrows: Minion Horde (5), Goblin Barrel (3), Skeleton Army (3). Their deck has 1 Arrows (3). You play threats faster than they cycle Arrows (4 cards). The threat that hits without Arrows deals massive damage. This is Arrows Bait — a variant of Log Bait that punishes the most common Arena 10 small spell.
