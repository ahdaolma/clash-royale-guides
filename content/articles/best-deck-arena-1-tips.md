## 10 Arena 1 Tips That Actually Work

![Clash Royale Goblin Stadium Arena 1](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.nh8Z1b31xTh6SAABbF-gQQHaL5%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=b9b5a2b9303a2b1620134763767cf4163dbfb57d2db69a134fafb45e4d2281f3&ipo=images)

Arena 1 (Goblin Stadium, 0-400 trophies) has a unique meta: only 18 cards exist, nobody has buildings, and nobody has legendary cards. Most "beginner guides" give generic advice. Here are 10 Arena 1-specific tips that exploit the actual card pool to win 80%+ of your matches.

### 1. Giant Is Overpowered Because Nobody Has Buildings

In Arena 1, the only defensive buildings available are... none. Cannon unlocks in Arena 2, Tesla in Arena 4, Inferno Tower in Arena 5. Your opponents must spend elixir on troops to stop your Giant, and every troop they drop feeds your counter-push. A Giant placed behind the King Tower with a Musketeer behind it requires 5+ elixir to defend cleanly—and most Arena 1 players overcommit 7-8 elixir, giving you a free positive trade. Abuse this. Giant is the best win condition in Arena 1 by a wide margin.

### 2. Valkyrie MUST Be Placed ON TOP of Witch, Not In Front

![Valkyrie countering Witch in Arena 1](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse3.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.F7qb904g4MSqAApdre57wwAAAA%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=b17187f96a0626a9cb7fb6df95aa08cf70434fc3bf7646ad9c530a23dac71013&ipo=images)

Witch is the most-played card in Arena 1, appearing in roughly 40% of decks. Most new players drop Valkyrie in front of the Witch. Wrong. The Witch locks onto Valkyrie, and her spawned Skeletons surround her—Valkyrie spends 3-4 swings clearing skeletons instead of hitting the Witch. Place Valkyrie directly on top of the Witch: she instantly clears all existing skeletons with her 360-degree splash, then kills the Witch in 3 swings. The Witch never gets a second skeleton wave off. This single placement difference turns a Witch from "annoying" to "free elixir trade."

### 3. Save Arrows ONLY for Goblin Barrel and Minion Horde

![Arrow timing against Goblin Barrel](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse3.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.bs0pONhgn1IAw3bg0mGAqwAAAA%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=d01891a3dd7ea8c20bd1cf23fa358a6d7daf6359b1d1872ad283a49c13153c65&ipo=images)

Goblin Barrel is an Arena 1 unlock and Arena 1 players love it. They also love Minion Horde (Training Camp unlock). Both cards will shred your tower if unanswered. The rule: never use Arrows on anything else. Not on Spear Goblins at the bridge. Not on Skeleton Army. If your opponent plays Goblin Barrel and you Arrows it, that is a neutral trade (3 for 3), but their tower took 0 damage. If they play Minion Horde and you Arrows it, that is a +2 positive trade. If you waste Arrows on a 2-elixir card, they will punish with Barrel on the next cycle and you have no answer.

Advanced tip: if your opponent out-cycles your Arrows (drops Barrel, you Arrows, they cycle back to Barrel before your Arrows is in hand), use Valkyrie. Drop her directly on the tower when the Barrel shadow appears. She kills all three Goblins in one swing before they get a single stab.

### 4. Mini P.E.K.K.A on Defense, Then Counter-Push

Mini P.E.K.K.A is the best defensive card in Arena 1. At tournament standard, she deals 598 damage per swing—she three-shots a Giant (2,000 HP at level 1), one-shots a Musketeer, and two-shots a Witch. The key: use her on defense first, not offense. When their Giant crosses the bridge, drop Mini P.E.K.K.A behind it (between Giant and their support). She melts the Giant in 3 swings, then marches toward their tower with 80%+ HP. Drop Spear Goblins behind her for support. This 6-elixir counter-push (Mini P.E.K.K.A + Spear Goblins) demands a response, and your opponent just spent 5+ elixir on their failed push. They have nothing left.

### 5. Prince Is a Defensive Card, Not an Offensive Card

Most Arena 1 players see Prince's charge damage (651 at level 1) and immediately play him at the bridge. This loses games. Prince at the bridge is 5 elixir that dies to a 1-elixir Skeleton Army. Instead, use Prince defensively: when their Giant or Knight crosses the bridge, drop Prince behind them. He charges into their tank, kills it in 2-3 hits, then charges toward their tower with support behind him. A defensive Prince that becomes a counter-push is worth 10 elixir of value. An offensive Prince at the bridge is worth 0.

### 6. Count Your Opponent's Elixir—It Is Easier Than You Think

![Clash Royale card collection arena 1](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.jAMFrKOCAhxVI4p0Rmc39QAAAA%3Fpid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=aef23b14f3f95bb1cd3261f61cd94b07cd9e4d2734cf503dcf940f16b87251c1&ipo=images)

In Arena 1, elixir counting is simpler than at higher arenas because the card pool is small. Most decks cost 3.0-3.8 average elixir. Here is the cheat: when your opponent plays a card, note how much elixir it cost. Then add 3.5 for every 10 seconds that pass. If they played Giant (5) + Musketeer (4) + Arrows (3) in one push, that is 12 elixir. You know they are at 0-1 elixir. Your 5-elixir Giant at the opposite lane is essentially free tower damage—they cannot defend both lanes with no elixir. Elixir counting is not about exact numbers. It is about knowing when they are low enough that your push is unanswerable.

### 7. Skeleton Army Punishes Everything—But Only Once

Skeleton Army (3 elixir, 15 skeletons) is the ultimate Arena 1 punish card. It kills Giant, Prince, Mini P.E.K.K.A, Musketeer, Witch (if she is alone)—every ground troop in the card pool. But every Arena 1 player runs Arrows. The first time you play Skeleton Army, it will work. The second time, they will predict it and Arrow. The solution: play Skeleton Army exactly once per match for a game-winning defense, then never play it again. Use it when their Arrows is out of cycle (they just used it on your Goblin Barrel or Minion Horde). That one Skeleton Army can kill a 10-elixir push for 3 elixir and win the game.

### 8. Goblin Hut Is a Win Condition in Arena 1

Goblin Hut (5 elixir, spawns Spear Goblins every 4.5 seconds for 40 seconds) is an Arena 1 unlock that most players ignore. This is a mistake. Over its lifetime, a Goblin Hut spawns 8-9 waves of Spear Goblins—that is 500+ chip damage on a tower if unanswered. In Arena 1, where most decks lack a dedicated building-killer, the Hut sits behind the tower chipping away while you defend. By double elixir, you can have two Huts up, spawning Spear Goblins from both lanes. Your opponent panics, overcommits to killing a Hut, and you punish the other lane. The Hut is a passive win condition that requires zero skill to get value from—perfect for Arena 1.

### 9. Never Play First—Always React

This is the single highest-impact habit change in Arena 1. The player who plays first card is at an elixir disadvantage for the entire opening. If you play nothing and they play Giant behind King Tower (5 elixir), you now have 10 elixir to their 5 while the Giant walks. Drop Mini P.E.K.K.A on their Giant and Spear Goblins at the bridge opposite lane. They must defend both sides with 5 elixir. They cannot. If both players wait until 1:00, that is fine—you lose nothing. If you get impatient and drop a card first, you immediately hand them the advantage.

### 10. Bomber Behind Giant Is Your Best Push Under 3,000 Trophies

The Bomber (2 elixir) is the most underrated Arena 1 card. Behind a Giant, the Bomber kills Skeleton Army, Goblins, and Spear Goblins in one bomb each—all the cheap swarm defenses Arena 1 players rely on. Giant (5) + Bomber (2) + Musketeer (4) = 11 elixir push that is nearly unstoppable in Arena 1. The Bomber clears swarms so the Giant and Musketeer can focus the tower. If they Valkyrie your Bomber, the Musketeer kills Valkyrie. If they ignore the Bomber, he kills their entire defense. For 2 elixir, Bomber adds more push value than any other 2-elixir card in the game.
