## 10 Arena 4 Tips That Push You to Spell Valley

![Clash Royale PEKKA's Playhouse Arena 4](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthf.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.zOCCx94sPTMP04QZEINEGQHaEK%3Fr%3D0%26cb%3Dthfc1falcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=db9a1ee57e9e30dc830e5994f0fbd532520235710805b21ac82c5214934b7c2f&ipo=images)

PEKKA's Playhouse (1100-1400 trophies) is where Clash Royale gets real. Buildings enter the meta (Inferno Tower), Hog Rider becomes playable, and PEKKA herself turns defense into unstoppable offense. Most players get stuck here because they keep playing Arena 3 strategies into a meta that now has answers. Here are 10 tips specific to the Arena 4 card pool.

### 1. Hog Rider Is Now Your Best Win Condition—But Not at the Bridge

![Hog Rider in PEKKA's Playhouse](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthf.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.OdnY7-1xC7QGuLl3V2zzlAHaEK%3Fr%3D0%26cb%3Dthfc1falcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=a4f6c8415a3edca0d4ccc1b850b5d57220eede3f0a2220914699e3ec4ce102f8&ipo=images)

Hog Rider unlocks in Arena 4 and immediately becomes the most consistent win condition. At 4 elixir, 1,600 HP, and 318 damage per swing (level 1), a single Hog Rider that connects deals 636-954 tower damage depending on how quickly it is answered. The key: always pig-push. Place Hog Rider at the very outer edge of the bridge tile, not the center. The pig-push makes the Hog jump the river without walking to the bridge, avoiding building pulls placed at the standard 3-4 plant position. If your opponent has Inferno Tower, pig-push the opposite lane—the Inferno Tower range cannot reach the far-lane Hog, and they must spend elixir on troops.

### 2. Inferno Tower Changes Everything—Place It at 4-3

![Inferno Tower defensive placement](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthf.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.CsLW-b_I4O-okLFK_6i6yQHaEK%3Fr%3D0%26cb%3Dthfc1falcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=e314ab173310c2c99709d362ba332ed7770f47bc56cbbc56671bda8f999f604f&ipo=images)

Inferno Tower (5 elixir) is the first real defensive building most players unlock. It ramps up to 2,080 damage per second at max charge, shredding Giants, PEKKAs, Golems (once you face them), and Hog Riders. The optimal placement is 4 tiles from the river, 3 tiles from the Princess Tower—this is the "4-3 plant." It pulls both-lane tanks and sits outside spell range of most Fireball angles. Never place Inferno Tower at 3-2 (too close to the tower; a Fireball on the tower also clips the Inferno). Never place it at 5-2 (does not pull the far lane). The 4-3 plant is the standard defensive building placement for the rest of your Clash Royale career—learn it now.

One critical weakness: Inferno Tower is helpless against swarms. A Skeleton Army, Goblin Barrel, or Minion Horde on top of it will destroy it in 2-3 seconds because the Inferno beam can only target one unit at a time. Always have Arrows, Zap (unlocks Arena 5), or a splash troop ready to protect your Inferno Tower.

### 3. PEKKA on Defense, Never on Offense

PEKKA costs 7 elixir and deals 678 damage per swing at level 1. She two-shots Giants, three-shots Hog Riders, and one-shots Musketeers. The mistake: playing PEKKA at the bridge. A 7-elixir PEKKA at the bridge dies to a 3-elixir Skeleton Army while your opponent laughs and counter-pushes the other lane.

The correct play: PEKKA behind the King Tower on defense. When their Giant + support crosses the bridge, PEKKA is already walking. She kills the Giant in 2 swings, then marches toward their tower with 80%+ HP. Drop a Wizard or Musketeer behind her. This is the PEKKA counter-push—and in Arena 4, where most players lack the elixir management to defend a full-health PEKKA with support, it wins games. You spent 7 elixir on defense that became offense. Your opponent spent 8+ elixir on a push that died.

### 4. Freeze Is Not a Win Condition—It Is a Surprise Punish

![Freeze spell in Arena 4](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthf.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.5O7ogil_StanvWxLd1gVCAHaEK%3Fr%3D0%26cb%3Dthfc1falcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=5cc39a2d3a644a2229f8a8005d4d32d6800287d192d810fb22c6c9f191e59333&ipo=images)

Freeze (4 elixir) unlocks in Arena 4 and works exactly once per match. The first time you Freeze their tower and defending troops as your Hog Rider connects, they will not expect it, and you will get 1,500+ damage. The second time, they will spread their troops, pre-place buildings, and your Freeze will hit nothing.

The optimal Freeze deck in Arena 4: Hog Rider (4) + Freeze (4) + a cheap cycle. When your Hog crosses the bridge and they drop Minion Horde or Skeleton Army to defend, Freeze the tower AND the defending troops. The Hog gets 2-3 extra swings while everything is frozen. That is a 4-elixir spell that adds 600-900 tower damage—a better rate than Fireball (4 elixir, 229 tower damage at level 1). But again: only once. After the first Freeze, they will play around it. Switch to chip damage for the rest of the match.

### 5. Minion Horde Is High Risk, High Reward

Minion Horde (5 elixir, 6 minions) deals 504 DPS at level 1—enough to kill a Giant in 4 seconds, a Hog Rider in 3 seconds, and a PEKKA in 10 seconds (though PEKKA cannot target air, so the Horde is untouchable). It is the highest DPS card in Arena 4. But every Arena 4 deck runs Arrows or Fireball, and a spelled Minion Horde is a -2 elixir trade (5 elixir Horde dies to 3 elixir Arrows).

The rule: never play Minion Horde first. Wait until your opponent uses their spell. If they Arrow your Goblin Barrel or Fireball your Musketeer, their spell is out of cycle for 10-15 seconds. THAT is your Minion Horde window. Drop it on their tank, or—if they are low on elixir—at the bridge for a 5-elixir push that deals 1,000+ damage if unspelled.

### 6. Battle Ram Is a Budget Hog Rider That Also Blocks

Battle Ram (4 elixir) charges like Prince, targets buildings, and on impact spawns 2 Barbarians. In Arena 4, it is slept on. The Ram itself deals 392 charge damage to a tower, and the two Barbarians add ~300 more if unaddressed—roughly equivalent to a Hog Rider's damage output. But the Battle Ram has a bonus: it can block. Drop Battle Ram in front of your Musketeer, and the Ram tanks 2-3 tower shots while the Musketeer fires freely. The Ram also baits buildings—if they drop Inferno Tower for your Ram, the Ram dies, but now the Inferno Tower is placed and ticking down, and your actual win condition (Hog Rider) has a clear path.

### 7. The Arena 4 PEKKA Deck That Wins 65%+

![PEKKA deck composition Arena 4](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Fthf.bing.com%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.s1LBami3qmvXWWOVmfUjYAHaFv%3Fr%3D0%26cb%3Dthfc1falcon2%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=6ac1d614bf7dc846f5f65441fb54310e53ab97bd939282722f5b8d65bfbe4877&ipo=images)

Here is a proven Arena 4 deck using only cards available through Arena 4:

- PEKKA (7) — defensive tank killer, counter-push win condition
- Hog Rider (4) — secondary win condition, punishes opposite lane
- Musketeer (4) — ranged support, air defense
- Valkyrie (4) — splash tank, kills swarms
- Arrows (3) — swarm clear
- Fireball (4) — medium spell, tower finisher
- Spear Goblins (2) — cheap cycle, chip, air defense
- Skeletons (1) — cycle, distraction, positive trades

Average elixir: 3.6. The strategy: defend with PEKKA, counter-push with support. When they overcommit to stopping the PEKKA push, Hog Rider opposite lane. This deck has two win conditions, strong defense, and answers for everything in the Arena 4 meta.

### 8. Zap Unlocks in Arena 5—Until Then, Arrows Is Mandatory

This is not an Arena 4 tip per se, but it explains why Arena 4 is hard: you do not have Zap yet. Zap (2 elixir, instant, resets Inferno Tower and Sparky) unlocks in Arena 5 (Spell Valley). In Arena 4, your small spell is Arrows (3 elixir). This means you cannot reset Inferno Tower, you cannot stun Prince charges, and your cheapest spell is 1 elixir more expensive than Zap would be. Build your deck with this in mind: if your deck currently relies on Zap, swap it for Arrows and accept the 1-elixir tax. Once you hit Arena 5, swap back and your deck instantly becomes 1 elixir cheaper.

### 9. Opposite-Lane Pressure Beats Arena 4 Defenses

Arena 4 players are learning real defense. They have Inferno Tower. They have PEKKA. They have buildings. If you push the same lane repeatedly, they will defend it every time. The counter: push both lanes. Hog Rider left. They drop Inferno Tower left. Immediately PEKKA + Musketeer right. Their Inferno Tower is in the wrong lane. They have 5 elixir tied up in a building that is not defending the real push. Split-lane pressure is the single biggest skill gap between Arena 3 and Arena 4 players. Master it and you win.

### 10. Track Their Building Cycle

Inferno Tower costs 5 elixir and has a 5-card cycle. Your Hog Rider costs 4 elixir and cycles every 4-5 cards. If you track when they play Inferno Tower, you know when it is not in their hand. The moment they play Inferno Tower and it expires (30-second lifetime), you have roughly 15-20 seconds where they cannot play it again. Cycle to your Hog Rider. Punish. This is called "out-cycling their building," and it is how Hog Rider players win even when the opponent has a hard counter. The building is only a counter if it is in their hand when your Hog is in yours.
