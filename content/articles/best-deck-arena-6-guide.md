## Deck #1: X-Bow Tesla Siege (59% Win Rate in Arena 6)

X-Bow is the card that separates good players from great ones. At 6 elixir with 1,330 HP and a deploy time of 4 seconds, placing it at the wrong moment loses you the game on the spot. Placed correctly, an X-Bow lock on the tower deals 1,200+ damage in 10 seconds.

The fundamental question of X-Bow: **"Do they have a tank in hand?"** If they just played Giant, Golem, or Giant Skeleton, the answer is no — and you can X-Bow. If they are holding a tank, you must cycle to outrun it or play defensively.

### The Deck

| Card | Elixir | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| X-Bow | 6 | Win condition |
| Tesla | 4 | Defensive building / X-Bow protector |
| Archers | 3 | Air defense / cycle |
| Knight | 3 | Mini-tank for X-Bow |
| Fireball | 4 | Medium spell |
| The Log | 2 | Ground swarm clear |
| Ice Spirit | 1 | Cycle |
| Skeletons | 1 | Cycle |

**Average elixir: 3.0** — this is a cycle deck. You can cycle back to X-Bow in 8 cards (10 elixir minimum) with Skeletons + Ice Spirit + Log.

### Offensive X-Bow Placement

Place X-Bow **one tile behind the bridge, centered.** Not at the bridge — bridge placement lets them drop a tank directly on it. One tile back creates a buffer zone where you can place Tesla or Knight between your X-Bow and their tower, blocking their tank drop. This single tile is the difference between a dead X-Bow and a tower kill.

**The Tesla Angle:** Place Tesla one tile in front of X-Bow, offset toward the center. Tesla covers air (Balloon, Baby Dragon) and ground (Hog Rider, Giant) while hiding underground between shots. At 4 elixir, it is the best X-Bow protector in the game.

### When to X-Bow: The Tank Rule

Never X-Bow if they have a tank in cycle. The sequence:
1. Track their card cycle — mentally note every time they play Giant, Golem, or Giant Skeleton
2. Count four of their cards after the tank
3. When the tank is 4 cards deep in their cycle, X-Bow at the bridge
4. By the time they cycle back to tank, your X-Bow has already dealt 800+ damage

### When NOT to X-Bow (Defensive Mode)

If they out-cycle you or you misplay, switch to defensive X-Bow. Place X-Bow **between your towers, not at the bridge.** It now functions as a 1,330 HP defensive building that shreds tanks. Defensive X-Bow + Tesla stops a Golem push for 10 elixir (vs their 8+ elixir Golem plus support).

## Deck #2: Golem Beatdown (57% Win Rate)

![Golem beatdown deck](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.abc123&f=1)

Golem costs 8 elixir — the most expensive card in Arena 6. When you play Golem, you are at 2 elixir (from 10). Your opponent has 10 elixir and can punish you immediately. This is the Golem gamble: survive the punish, build a push that cannot be stopped.

Golem's death damage (346) kills Minions and Goblins around it. Golemites (864 HP each, 72 death damage) provide residual tanking after the main body dies. Total HP pool: 4,256 + 864 + 864 = **5,984 HP.** That is the highest HP-per-elixir in the game at 748 HP/elixir (compare Giant: 3,344/5 = 669 HP/elixir).

### The Deck

| Card | Elixir | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Golem | 8 | Win condition / tank |
| Baby Dragon | 4 | Air splash behind Golem |
| Mega Minion | 3 | Air DPS |
| Zap | 2 | Reset / swarm clear |
| Elixir Collector | 6 | Elixir advantage |
| Barbarians | 5 | Defense |
| Archers | 3 | Air defense / cycle |
| Fireball | 4 | Medium spell |

**Average elixir: 4.4** — heavy, but Elixir Collector offsets it.

### The Golem Placement Rule

Always place Golem **behind your King Tower, in the corner.** Never at the bridge (8 elixir down, zero support). The corner placement gives you 12 seconds before Golem crosses the bridge — enough time to regenerate from 2 elixir back to 8-10 elixir for support troops.

### Elixir Collector First? The Risk Matrix

Placing Elixir Collector (6 elixir) as your first move is high-risk, high-reward:
- **If they don't punish:** You gain +2 elixir advantage over 70 seconds. Worth it.
- **If they X-Bow opposite lane:** You have 4 elixir vs their 6-elixir X-Bow. You lose a tower.
- **If they Hog Rider + spell:** You have 4 elixir to defend. Survivable but painful.

The safer opener: wait until 10 elixir. If they play nothing, Golem behind King Tower. If they play a 4+ elixir card, place Collector in front of King Tower (it tanks for King) and defend with remaining elixir.

### Defending After Golem: The 2-Elixir Survival Kit

You just spent 8 on Golem. You have 2 elixir. Their most common punish:
- Hog Rider (4) opposite lane: Zap + Skeletons delays it 2 seconds, you take 1 hit (400 damage). Acceptable.
- X-Bow (6) opposite lane: Drop Barbarians on it. You take 0 damage. They wasted 6 elixir.
- Balloon (5): Archers + Zap. Balloon dies before reaching tower.

## Deck #3: Mortar Bait Cycle (58% Win Rate)

![Mortar siege deck](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.abc123&f=1)

Mortar is X-Bow's cheaper cousin — 4 elixir vs 6, 220 area damage per shot, same 11.5 tile range. It does not demand the same commitment as X-Bow, making it more forgiving. Miss a Mortar? You lost 4 elixir. Miss an X-Bow? You lost 6 and probably a tower.

### The Deck

| Card | Elixir | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Mortar | 4 | Win condition / defensive building |
| Skeleton Barrel | 3 | Secondary win condition / bait |
| Inferno Tower | 5 | Tank defense |
| Archers | 3 | Air defense |
| Knight | 3 | Mini-tank |
| Zap | 2 | Small spell |
| Fireball | 4 | Medium spell |
| Ice Spirit | 1 | Cycle |

**Average elixir: 3.1** — faster than the X-Bow variant.

### Mortar Placement: The Blind Spot Tile

Place Mortar **centered one tile behind the bridge.** From this position:
- It hits the tower (11.5 range covers 11 tiles to tower)
- Enemy melee troops must walk around the river to reach it
- You can plant Inferno Tower between Mortar and river to block tanks
- The blind spot (minimum range) is only 3.5 tiles — most troops don't reach it before dying

### Defensive Mortar: The Hidden Value

Mortar costs 4 elixir but has offensive AND defensive value. When you cannot lock onto their tower (they have a tank), play Mortar **centered between your towers.** It becomes a splash-damage defensive building that clears support troops behind their tank. Four defensive Mortars in a match provide more value than one offensive lock — this is the secret that top Mortar players know.
