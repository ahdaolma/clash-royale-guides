## 10 PEKKA Bridge Spam Tips for Relentless Wins

![PEKKA Bridge Spam deck](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse2.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.9SBEyg0Irza-hKqSR78cjAHaDt%3Fr%3D0%26pid%3DApi&f=1&ipt=08c620da85ff720bae2b1260df2586996a495befd1874f49897a7dea5c2af920&ipo=images)

PEKKA Bridge Spam is misnamed. The PEKKA (7 elixir) is not your win condition — she is your defensive anchor that becomes a counter-push. The real weapons are Battle Ram, Bandit, and Royal Ghost — the bridge-spam trio that punishes every moment of weakness.

---

### 1. PEKKA Is Defense First, Offense Second

Never play PEKKA at the bridge or behind King Tower as a first move. She costs 7 elixir, walks at Slow speed, and deals 510 damage per hit. She is the best tank-killer in the game. Her role: defend their Golem/Giant/Royal Giant/Mega Knight. Kill their tank. Survive with 60%+ HP. NOW she walks toward their tower and they must answer a 7-elixir threat with zero elixir. That is PEKKA Bridge Spam. The "Spam" comes AFTER the "PEKKA" defense.

### 2. Battle Ram: The Real Win Condition

Battle Ram (4 elixir) charges after 3.5 tiles, dealing 472 charge damage + 294 per Barbarian hit. A fully connected Battle Ram deals 1,060 tower damage. The key: Battle Ram must be played when their building (Tombstone, Tesla, Inferno Tower) is out of cycle. Track their building. When it is 3rd or 4th in their hand, Battle Ram at the bridge connects. Pair with Bandit (3) in the opposite lane for dual-lane pressure. One lane will break.

### 3. Bandit Dashes Through Punish Windows

Bandit (3 elixir) is invulnerable during her dash. This means she dashes THROUGH Mega Knight's spawn damage, THROUGH Sparky's shot, THROUGH Wizard's fireball. Timing: when their Mega Knight is about to land, Bandit at the bridge. MK's spawn damage misses entirely. Bandit connects for 256 damage + 320 dash damage. The Bandit dash is a dodge mechanic. Use it defensively: Bandit into their support troop to tank and kill while invulnerable for 0.5 seconds.

### 4. Royal Ghost: The Invisible Pressure

Royal Ghost (3 elixir) turns invisible after 1.6 seconds of not attacking. Invisible Ghost cannot be targeted by troops or towers. At the bridge, Ghost deals 216 DPS and forces a reaction. They drop Goblins — Ghost kills them in two swings. They ignore him — Ghost deals 432 damage before revealing. The Ghost is your cheapest bridge-spam tool and your scout. Play him at the bridge to see what they have. If they overcommit, you know their hand. If they ignore, free damage.

### 5. Electro Wizard: The Zap-on-a-Stick

Electro Wizard (4 elixir) stuns on spawn AND every attack. Against Inferno Tower: drop E-Wiz 5 tiles from the tower. His spawn zap resets the beam. Every attack (1.8 sec interval) resets it again. Inferno Tower never reaches tier 2 beam. Against Sparky: E-Wiz permanently stun-locks her. Against Balloon: E-Wiz stuns it mid-air, delaying the bomb drop by 0.5 seconds per attack. E-Wiz is the reason PEKKA Bridge Spam beats Inferno Tower, Sparky, and Balloon decks.

### 6. Poison Over Fireball: The Chip Difference

PEKKA Bridge Spam traditionally runs Poison (4 elixir) over Fireball. The reason: Poison lasts 8 seconds, dealing 75 DPS for a total of 600 damage — AND it damages troops that walk through it. Against Graveyard: Poison kills every Skeleton that spawns for 8 seconds. Against Elixir Collector: Poison denies 4 of the 8 elixir and deals tower damage. Against Musketeer/Wizard behind a tank: Poison hits both the support AND the tower. The sustained damage over 8 seconds out-values Fireball's instant burst in bridge-spam decks where chip matters.

### 7. Zap + Battle Ram: The 6-Elixir Guaranteed Connection

Battle Ram (4) at the bridge. They drop Skeleton Army (3). You Zap (2) instantly. Skeletons die. Ram connects. 6 elixir for 1,060 tower damage. This is the simplest winning combo in the deck. But it only works if you have Zap in hand AND they have swarm in hand. The tip: never Battle Ram without Zap ready. If you just cycled Zap, wait. If you Battle Ram into Skeleton Army without Zap, you fed them +1 and your push is dead. Patience. Zap + Ram always.

### 8. Magic Archer: Geometry Wins Games

Magic Archer (4 elixir) pierces through ALL targets in a line for 111 damage per shot at 1.1 second fire rate. Behind a PEKKA counter-push, Magic Archer at the back lines up the tower AND every troop they place to defend. Each shot: 111 to their troop + 111 to the tower. Over a 10-second push: 10 shots = 1,110 tower damage just from Magic Archer pierce. The positioning: place him DIRECTLY behind PEKKA in the same lane. Every troop they drop to stop PEKKA lines up perfectly for pierce damage.

### 9. The Opposite-Lane Dual Push

PEKKA defended their tank and is walking toward left tower. Immediately Battle Ram + Bandit at the RIGHT bridge. They must choose: spend elixir to stop PEKKA (7 elixir value walking at them) or spend elixir to stop Battle Ram + Bandit (7 elixir combined, but faster). Most players choose wrong. They split their defense and both pushes connect for partial damage. The key: the dual push must happen while PEKKA is crossing the bridge. If you wait until PEKKA dies, the window closes.

### 10. Against Cycle Decks: Spell Cycle Back

PEKKA Bridge Spam struggles against fast cycle decks (Hog 2.6, Log Bait) because they out-cycle your expensive cards. The counter: stop playing PEKKA. Defend with Bandit (3), Royal Ghost (3), and Electro Wizard (4). Spell cycle with Poison (4) + Zap (2). PEKKA (7) stays in hand for their tank push only. Against cycle decks, treat PEKKA Bridge Spam as a 3.0-average control deck, not a beatdown deck. Adapt the playstyle to the matchup.

---

## Key Takeaway

PEKKA Bridge Spam wins by converting defense into offense. Every PEKKA defense is a potential tower takedown. Every Bandit dash is a dodge AND a punish. Every Battle Ram is a building-tracker check. Play defense first. The offense comes free.
