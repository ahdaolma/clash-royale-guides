## 10 Arena 6 Tips That Break You Into Royal Arena

![Builder's Workshop tips](https://external-content.duckduckgo.com/iu/?u=https%3A%2F%2Ftse1.mm.bing.net%2Fth%2Fid%2FOIP.D9oHcRxA5aEAtPF_oFqfZgHaFW&f=1)

### 1. The X-Bow Tank Count Rule

Before playing X-Bow, answer one question: "Where is their tank?" Count their last four cards. If Giant, Golem, or Giant Skeleton is among them, do not X-Bow. If the tank is buried 5+ cards deep in their cycle, X-Bow immediately — by the time they cycle back to tank, your X-Bow has done 800+ tower damage.

### 2. X-Bow Bridge vs Back Placement: Know the Difference

Bridge X-Bow (offensive): one tile behind the bridge, not at it. This creates space for Tesla/Knight to block tank drops. Back X-Bow (defensive): between your towers. A defensive X-Bow has 1,330 HP and shreds any tank that crosses — often a better play than offensive when you are behind.

### 3. Never Waste Tesla Before X-Bow

Tesla costs 4 elixir and lasts 35 seconds. If you cycle Tesla on defense, then play X-Bow (6 elixir), your Tesla has 20 seconds left and will expire mid-lock. Always ensure at least 25 seconds remain on Tesla before committing X-Bow. If not, cycle Archers or Skeletons for defense instead.

### 4. Golem in the Corner, Never at the Bridge

Golem in the back corner behind King Tower gives you 12 seconds of elixir regeneration before it crosses the bridge — from 2 elixir to 9-10. Golem at the bridge means you have 2 elixir to support an 8-elixir tank. You lose. Always corner.

### 5. Elixir Collector After Their First Move

Never play Elixir Collector (6 elixir) as your opening card. If they immediately X-Bow or Hog Rider opposite lane, you have 4 elixir to defend and take 1,000+ tower damage. Wait until they play a 4+ elixir card first, then Collector in front of King Tower (it absorbs some damage while pumping).

### 6. Rocket Math: When to Spell Cycle

Rocket deals 1,232 area damage at tournament standard for 6 elixir. That is over half a tower's HP (2,534). In overtime, if their tower is at or below 1,232 HP, Rocket it. You need to land two Rockets total — one during regular time, one in overtime. Most Arena 6 players do not track their tower HP and lose winnable matches to chip damage.

### 7. Mortar Placement: The One-Tile-Back Secret

Mortar placed at the bridge is vulnerable to any tank dropped directly on it — Giant, Knight, Valkyrie. Placed one tile back, melee troops must path around the river. This adds 2 seconds to their walk time — two extra Mortar shots (440 damage). That tiny tile is the difference between pressure and wasted elixir.

### 8. Inferno Tower Does NOT Counter X-Bow

Inferno Tower ramps to 1,760 DPS. But X-Bow outranges it — 11.5 tiles vs Inferno's 6 tile range. An X-Bow at the bridge shoots your Inferno Tower before Inferno can lock onto X-Bow. The actual X-Bow counter: Rocket (one-shots at +1 level) or a tank dropped directly on it.

### 9. Tesla's Hidden State Is Its Real Value

Tesla hides underground when no enemies are nearby. This makes it immune to spells — Fireball, Poison, Arrows cannot damage a hidden Tesla. When defending against chip decks, Tesla provides 35 seconds of invisible, spell-proof defense for 4 elixir. That is the best defensive value in Arena 6.

### 10. Track Their Elixir by Watching Their Cycle

Arena 6 is where elixir tracking becomes mandatory. Simple method: every time they play a card, note its cost. When they play three 4+ elixir cards in a row, they are at 0-2 elixir. That is your window. X-Bow, Mortar, or Golem push immediately. Most Arena 6 players leak elixir for 3-5 seconds after a heavy sequence — that is free damage.
